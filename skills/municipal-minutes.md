# Municipal Minutes

A "proof of concept" skill to be expanded upon as part of project to create weekly "snapshots" of various municipal agency actvity from various towns.

## What this skill does

When invoked with `/municipal-minutes`, Claude will 

1. Download all the minutes and agendas from the municipal agency posted during the last 28 days as found on this page at the prompt.
2. In the neutral style of an AP journalist, write an article summarizing all the activity for a hyperlocal audience in the town represented by the agency. 
3. Include a headline that is optimized for search engines (≤109 characters, capitalize the first letter of each word)
4. Include a meta description (less than 136 characters)

## Rules

1. Use style rules as defined in the newsroom-style.md file

## Example interaction

**User:** /municipal-minutes https://clintonct.org/AgendaCenter/Town-Council-88/

**Claude:** Headline: Clinton Town Council Sends $68 Million Budget To May 13 Referendum,
Rejects School Resource Officer

Meta description: Clinton voters will decide on a $68M budget at referendum
May 13 after the council rejected a student resource officer 5-1.

---

CLINTON, CT — The Town Council sent a combined $68 million spending plan to a May
13 referendum on April 20 after hearing from two dozen residents and rejecting
a proposal to fund a school-based police officer.

The council voted to recommend a town government budget of $23,237,935 and a
Board of Education budget of $44,788,204 for the fiscal year beginning July 1,
2026. The town government figure reflects $136,000 in spending cuts approved
that evening; the proposed total before adjustments had stood at $23,373,935.

Two dozen residents address the council

Twenty-three residents spoke during a public hearing that opened at 7 p.m. in
the Town Hall Green Room. Most comments focused on a proposed Student Resource
Officer position. One resident raised concerns about the grand list and
recent property assessments; another expressed broad support for both budgets.
The hearing, presided over by Chairman Hank Teskey, adjourned at 7:50 p.m.
SRO funding rejected 5-1

When the council convened as a special meeting at 8 p.m., council member
Jeremiah Dunn moved to restore $155,000 in funding for the Police Student
Resource Officer. The motion failed 5-1, with Dunn casting the sole vote in
favor. Voting against were Carrie Allen, Dennis Donovan, Chris Passante, Brian
Roccapriore and Chairman Teskey.

Assessment appeals force mid-year adjustments
The Board of Assessment Appeals issued an estimated $11 million in assessment
reductions, producing a projected current-year tax revenue loss of $249,721.
To offset the shortfall, the council unanimously approved a package of
adjustments recommended by Town Manager Michelle Benivegna and Finance
Director Robert Tait.

The $136,000 in spending cuts included:
- Health Insurance Medical Contingency: -$50,000
- Health Insurance Consultant: -$50,000
- Workers' Compensation Insurance: -$16,000
- Police Overtime: -$10,000
- Public Works Repairs and Maintenance: -$10,000
The remaining $113,721 was recovered through projected revenue increases: an
$89,721 Town Aid Road Grant, $15,000 in miscellaneous receipts and $9,000 in
planning and zoning fees.

Fireworks display gets dedicated account

The council unanimously approved creating a new "Town Fireworks Display"
expenditure line and moving $10,000 from the community events account into it.
Under the resolution, those funds would be available to support the annual
fireworks if a volunteer committee falls short of what is needed to cover
costs.

Mill rate to drop sharply in revaluation year

Because 2026-27 is a revaluation year, the tentative mill rate is set to fall
from 31.14 to 22.55 — a decrease of about 27.6%. The town's net taxable grand
list grew from approximately $1.74 billion to $2.56 billion as a result of the
revaluation, meaning lower rates do not necessarily translate into lower
individual tax bills depending on how a given property was assessed.

The tax levy, including credits, is projected at $57,757,344, against a total
combined budget of approximately $68 million funded by local taxes, state
grants, fees and a $1 million draw from the fund balance.

What's next

Clinton residents will vote on the proposed town government and Board of
Education budgets at referendum on Wednesday, May 13, 2026. The full budget
book is available at clintonct.org.

Council members present April 20: Chairman Hank Teskey, Carrie Allen, Chris
Passante, Dennis Donovan, Brian Roccapriore and Jeremiah Dunn. Absent: Chris
Aniskovich.
